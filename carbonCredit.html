Key Points About Carbon Credits:

  • Purpose: Used to offset carbon emissions by supporting projects that reduce or remove an equivalent amount of GHGs.
  • 1 Credit = 1 Ton: One carbon credit represents the reduction or removal of one metric ton of CO2 or its equivalent in other GHGs (CO2e).
  • Compliance and Voluntary Markets:
    • Compliance Markets: Governed by regulations, companies must comply with emission caps set by regulatory bodies (e.g., EU ETS).
    • Voluntary Markets: Companies and individuals voluntarily purchase carbon credits to offset emissions for CSR goals or personal values.
  • Cap-and-Trade System: Governments set a cap on total emissions allowed, and companies can buy or sell carbon credits based on their emissions.
  • Projects Generating Carbon Credits: Projects like renewable energy, reforestation, and methane capture are certified to generate carbon credits.
  • Certification and Verification: Carbon credits are certified by third-party organizations to ensure emission reductions are real, measurable, additional, and permanent.
